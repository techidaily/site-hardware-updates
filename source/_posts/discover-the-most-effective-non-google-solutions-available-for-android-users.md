##  Google's Apps Come With the Play Store

 In order for an Android phone to include the Google Play Store, the manufacturer must include a handful of Google apps. That's why nearly every Android device comes pre-loaded with Google Search, Gmail, Chrome, YouTube, and other Google apps.

 We've taken the most popular of that bundle of apps and recommended some great alternatives. You may not be able to completely remove Google, but this is a start.

![Microsoft Outlook logo](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2020/11/microsoft-office-logo-new.jpg) 

 Gmail is a behemoth in the email game, but there are some solid alternatives. We're going with Microsoft again this time. [Outlook](https://extra-skills.techidaily.com/updated-realistic-quantum-leaps-a-vfx-perspective/) is a solid email service and [the Android app](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU208517/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.microsoft.office.outlook) is really nice.

##  Best Alternative to Google Maps on Android

![OsmAnd logo.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/09/OsmAnd.png) 

 There are not many services that can rival Google Maps. The best alternative is probably Apple Maps, but you don't have an iPhone. [OsmAnd](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU208517/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=net.osmand) is a serviceable contender worth a look.

 One of the best features of OsmAnd is offline maps. It'll ask you at the start where you live and download a large area around that location. OsmAnd is open-source and based on OpenStreetMap. It's a little clunkier than Google Maps, but it packs a lot of features.

##  Best Alternative to Google Drive on Android

![Dropbox Hero Image](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2020/04/dropbox_hero_2.jpg) 

 Before Google Drive came around, [Dropbox](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU208517/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.dropbox.android) was the king of cloud storage. The service is still kicking, and it's still a good alternative. Dropbox is priced similarly to Google Drive, offering a whopping 2TB of storage for $9.99 per month.

 It lacks some of the collaborating features you can find in Google Drive, but the interface is less messy, [it can sync with your PC](https://fox-boxes.techidaily.com/new-zero-cost-green-screens-for-professionals/), and it's easy to share files with other people. It's a solid place to store all of your random files and stuff.
