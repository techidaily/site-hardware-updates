## Pre-Order Now: The Revolutionary 8BitDo Retro 18 Keyboard - Available Models for NES/Famicom & More Coming This July

8BitDo has made its[Retro 18 Mechanical Numpad](https://target.georiot.com/Proxy.ashx?tsid=45723&GR%5FURL=https%3A%2F%2Famazon.com%2Fdp%2FB0D4QC8R4P%2F%3Ftag%3Dhawk-future-20%26ascsubtag%3Dtomshardware-us-1019741722698360109-20) available for preorder on[Amazon](https://tools.techidaily.com) at $44.99\. This number pad pairs perfectly with the firm's own[NES-inspired keyboard](https://tools.techidaily.com) , which is only available in a TKL layout. Aside from working as a mechanical number pad, the Retro 18 also sports a retro LED display, and wireless connectivity, allowing you to use it as a standalone calculator.

 The Retro 18 is compatible with both Windows and Android, so you can use it on your laptop and smartphone. It has Bluetooth, 2.4G, and a USB-C port, giving you the flexibility to choose your preferred connection. The 2.4G dongle slots neatly and securely into the magnetic compartment at the bottom of the numpad, ensuring you will never lose it. Unfortunately, it’s not yet certified for macOS, iPads, and iPhones, so you must find an alternative if you’re an[Apple fan](https://tools.techidaily.com) .

 Aside from the screen and the number keys, you also get the following buttons on the top row: a themed CAL button to turn on calculator mode, a dedicated Windows calculator launch key, a wireless connection button, and a rotary knob to select its connection mode. As with the 8BitDo retro mechanical keyboards, this mechanical numpad also uses hot-swappable switches and supports N-key rollover. It uses Kailh Box White Switches V2 and PBT or double-shot ABS keycaps, all mounted on an aluminum plate for durability and stability.
