##  Make a Samsung Phone Display the Battery Percentage

 On a Samsung phone running Android 11 or 12, first, launch the Settings app. Then, navigate to Notifications > Advanced Settings.

![Tap &quot;Advanced Settings.&quot;](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/5-samsung-android-advanced-settings.png) 

 If you're on Android 10 (learn [how to check your Android version](https://sound-issues.techidaily.com/troubleshooting-sound-issues-in-windows-solutions-for-audio-pop-ups/)), you'll head into Settings > Notifications > Status Bar.

 Then, toggle on the "Show Battery Percentage" option.

![Turn on "Show Battery Percentage."](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2022/08/6-samsung-android-show-battery-percentage.png) 

 You now have your [current battery levels](https://extra-tips.techidaily.com/new-comparative-analysis-64-vs-128gb-video-holding-power/) shown in your screen's top-right corner. To hide it again, turn off the "Show Battery Percentage" option.
