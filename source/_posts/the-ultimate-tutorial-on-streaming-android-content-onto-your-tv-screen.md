##  What Is Screen Mirroring?

 Screen mirroring—also known as screen sharing or screen casting—is the act of displaying a live view of your phone's screen onto an external display (usually a TV). Everything you do on the phone is shown on the TV in real-time.

##  How to Cast Android to TV

 To "cast" your Android phone screen, you'll need a TV with a Chromecast or a Chromecast-enabled streaming device, such as a Google TV stick. The first thing to do is open the [Google Home](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU211553/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.google.android.apps.chromecast.app&hl=en%5FUS&gl=US) app and long-press your TV to open it.

![Long-press your TV.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/screenshot_20240417-163046.png) 

 Next, tap the "Cast My Screen" shortcut from the bottom of the screen.

![Cast My Screen.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/screenshot_20240417-163053.png) 

 Tap "Cast Screen" on the pop-up dialog.

![Select Cast Screen.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/screenshot_20240417-163103.png)

##  How to Mirror Android to Roku

 Mirroring an Android screen to [Roku](https://extra-approaches.techidaily.com/in-2024-step-by-step-integrating-songs-in-microsofts-presentation-maker/) requires a device that supports [Miracast](https://fix-guide.techidaily.com/simple-solutions-to-fix-android-systemui-has-stopped-error-for-poco-f5-pro-5g-drfone-by-drfone-fix-android-problems-fix-android-problems/). Most Android devices—with the exception of Google Pixel phones—support Miracast. It may be found under a number of different names:

* Smart View
* Quick Connect
* SmartShare
* AllShare Cast
* Wireless display
* Display mirroring
* HTC Connect
* Screen Casting

 First, you need to make sure [screen mirroring is enabled on the Roku](https://some-knowledge.techidaily.com/new-from-zero-to-hero-navigating-youtubes-growth-journey/). It should be enabled by default, but we can double-check by going to Settings > System > Screen Mirroring > Screen Mirroring Mode. You can choose to be prompted to allow screen mirroring every time or always allow it.

![Roku screen mirroring options.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/2023_03_13-10_56-AM-Office-Lens-1.jpg) 

 Next, it's a matter of finding your device's Miracast button in the Quick Settings menu. We'll be using a Samsung Galaxy device, which is called "Smart View." You may need to [edit the Quick Settings panel](https://youtube-blog.techidaily.com/ed-unlocking-video-popularity-a-comprehensive-guide-to-tagging-on-youtube-for-2024/) to find the button.

 Swipe down from the top of the screen once or twice—depending on your device—and select the Miracast button. In this case, it's labeled "Smart View."

![Tap the "Smart View" button.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2021/07/2023-03-13_11-04-44.jpg) 

 A menu will appear with devices connected to the same Wi-Fi network you're using. Select your Roku.

![Select the Roku.](https://static1.howtogeekimages.com/wordpress/wp-content/uploads/2024/04/screenshot_20230313_110643_smart-view.jpeg)

##  How to Mirror Android to Windows or Mac

 The simplest way to mirror an Android device to a Windows or Mac PC is with an app called AirDroid. There's an AirDroid app for your Android device and a companion web app/desktop app for the PC. [AirDroid](https://www.airdroid.com/) has been around for a long time, and it works great.

 First, install the "[AirDroid Cast](https://www.anrdoezrs.net/links/3607085/type/dlg/sid/UUhtgUeUpU211553/https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.sand.aircast)" app on your Android device. If you want to use the desktop app instead of the web app, install the [Windows](https://srv3.airdroid.com/p20/web/getbinaryredirect?type=cast%5Fexe&channel=&version=) or [Mac](https://srv3.airdroid.com/p20/web/getbinaryredirect?type=cast%5Fdmg&channel=&version=) version on your PC as well.
