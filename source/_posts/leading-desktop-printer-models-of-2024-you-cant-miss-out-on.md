##  What to Look For in a Printer

 With so many printers to choose from, it can be difficult to narrow your search. Luckily, there are a few key things to consider to help you find the best printer. The most important question to ask yourself is: What do I need my printer to do?

 Some printers are only capable of printing, while others are called all-in-one printers because they can scan, copy, and sometimes fax in addition to printing. Furthermore, some printers can only print in black-and-white, which is perfect for those who plan to print simple text documents, while others can print in color or even on special photo paper.

 Once you have your basic printer needs nailed down, you can start comparing the two main types of printers‚Äîlaser and inkjet. Laser printers have a more expensive entry cost but a lower Cost Per Page (CPP) with toner cartridges. Conversely, inkjet printers have a cheaper initial cost, but replacing ink cartridges results in a higher CPP than laser printers. Inkjet printers are the most common choice for home use, while laser printers are more common for business use.

 To determine CPP for yourself while printer shopping, find the cost of a replacement cartridge for the printer you're looking at and divide it by the number of estimated pages it'll yield. The number of pages you'll get will vary based on what you're printing, but it's a good gauge for that printer's average CPP.

 You should also consider which devices you want to print from. If you want to print wirelessly from a laptop or your smartphone, you'll need to ensure your printer has Wi-Fi support. Without Wi-Fi, a printer can connect to devices via USB or Ethernet, so it's not an essential feature, but it's nice to have.

 Finally, if you need to print a lot of pages, it's worth looking at a printer's capacity and print speed specs. For personal use, the average print speed and capacity of most printers is plenty. But if you frequently need to print 200 page documents or 100 shipping labels at the beginning of the day, a fast printer with a large paper capacity can make all the difference. Print speed is represented as Page Per Minute (PPM), and most printer manufacturers will list this as a main spec in the listing's description.
